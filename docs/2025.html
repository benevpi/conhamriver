Conham 2025: E. coli & enterococci, CSO spilling, rain, temperature & CAPE

Weekly E. coli and intestinal enterococci samples over daily upstream CSO spilling (same day, and trailing 2- and 7-day cumulative sums), daily rainfall, peak hourly rainfall, mean temperature and peak CAPE, May–December 2025. Wind isn't in the fetched data yet — see the note below.

Both E. coli and intestinal enterococci are on the top panel, capped at 1000 CFU/100ml (the lab maximum, dashed line). The green→red bands are UK bathing-water classes (Bathing Water Directive, inland waters): Excellent ≤200, Good ≤400, Sufficient ≤800, Poor above. They use the stricter (lower) of the two indicators — intestinal enterococci — so a reading's band is the tougher standard it must clear; the E. coli limits (≤500 / ≤1000) are looser. The Sufficient/Poor split above the Good limit is a single-sample approximation (the Directive classifies over multi-year percentiles, not single samples). CSO = hours of upstream spilling, shown every day: on the day itself, and as trailing 2-day and 7-day cumulative sums (all three panels share one y-axis, so the longer windows are always ≥ the shorter ones). Daily totals sum every upstream outfall's spill hours, so a single day can exceed 24. Rain & temperature are ERA5 daily values for Conham. Peak rainfall is the heaviest single hour (mm/hour) anywhere in the Bristol Avon catchment that day — a marker for localised, potentially convective downpours that daily totals hide. Peak CAPE is the highest hourly CAPE (convective/thunderstorm instability) anywhere in the Bristol Avon catchment that day — high CAPE means storm-favourable air. Hover for values. Source: docs/data/conham_2025_timeseries.csv.
